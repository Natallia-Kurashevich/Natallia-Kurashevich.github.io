Report

Methodology

Our methodology is designed to systematically explore and enhance the knowledge of Giorgio Morandi’s landscapes. The key components of our methodology include utilizing ArCo to gather detailed metadata on Morandi and his works, querying databases with SPARQL to extract specific information and leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to enrich the knowledge graph with contextual information that enhance the understanding of the paintings.

Objectives

  • To Explore: Investigate the information that concerns Giorgio Morandi and his paintings "Paesaggio. Grizzana" and "View of the Montagnola of Bologna, Glimpse of a Garden".
  • To Enhance Knowledge: Use SPARQL queries to collect and structure information. Enrich the existing knowledge graph with new data and insights generated by LLMs, thereby providing a deeper understanding of Morandi’s work. Create new knowledge triples.

Work Process

Stage 1 - Data Exploration and SPARQL Query Formulation

We formulated SPARQL queries to extract detailed information about Morandi and his paintings.

Stage 2 - Semantic Enrichment with LLMs and creation of new Knowledge Triples for Giorgio Morandi’s painting "Paesaggio. Grizzana"

We enriched the knowledge graph with new data generated by LLMs, enhancing our understanding of the painting.

Stage 3 - Semantic Enrichment with LLMs and creation of new Knowledge Triples for Giorgio Morandi’s painting "View of the Montagnola of Bologna, Glimpse of a Garden"

Similar to Stage 2, we enriched the knowledge graph with new data generated by LLMs for this painting.

Problems & Solutions

  • Creating properly running SPARQL queries. The first problem that we faced was creating accurate SPARQL queries. For instance, in one of the queries, SELECT DISTINCT didn’t help to eliminate duplicates. With the help of the website Stack Overflow, a solution for the problem was found. https://stackoverflow.com/questions/28988702/distinct-only-on-one-value-with-sparql

Conclusion

Our project achieved its primary objectives by successfully formulating SPARQL queries, utilizing Large Language Models, and creating new knowledge triples. This systematic approach allowed us to significantly enhance the ArCo knowledge graph, providing a more comprehensive understanding of Giorgio Morandi’s "Paesaggio. Grizzana" and "View of the Montagnola of Bologna, Glimpse of a Garden."
